Relational Positioning System

RPS = Relational Positioning System

A general computational lens for families, teams, friendships, mentorship, and partnerships—not only romance.

RPS Core combines three planes—Internal State, Power Dynamics, and Motivational Structure—as projections of how need circulates between you and another system.

You move a point on each plane and refine it with anchored sliders so structure becomes visible, discussable, and adjustable.

Three positioning planes

Plane 1 · Internal State

Locates how strongly you register being needed against how steadily you can stay regulated when relational signals are ambiguous.

What it shows

Where you sit between feeling needed and staying emotionally steady when signals are mixed.

Plane 2 · Power Dynamics

Shows whether influence flows evenly, leans on your pursuit, or concentrates as leverage—using paired directions of need.

What it shows

Whether need runs in both directions—or tilts toward pursuit, advantage, or distance.

Plane 3 · Motivational Structure

Captures how intensely the bond pulls on you, and whether continuity is held more by practical function or by meaning and attachment.

What it shows

How much pull you feel, and whether what keeps you connected leans practical or deeply personal.
